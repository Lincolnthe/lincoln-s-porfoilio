Certifications

OSHA 10 Hour general industry

The OSHA 10-Hour General Industry Certification is a training program developed by the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) in the United States. This certification is designed to provide workers with fundamental knowledge about workplace safety and health standards within the general industry sector. The course typically covers topics such as hazard recognition, emergency planning, ergonomics, personal protective equipment, and other essential safety measures. Participants undergo 10 hours of comprehensive training, delivered by authorized OSHA trainers. The program aims to empower workers with the skills to identify and mitigate workplace hazards, fostering a safer and healthier work environment. While the certification is not mandatory in all industries, many employers require or strongly recommend it to enhance employee awareness and compliance with safety regulations. Completing the OSHA 10-Hour General Industry Certification demonstrates a commitment to workplace safety and can be a valuable asset for individuals pursuing careers in various industries.
